One model, MCP, a few API calls. That handles most things today and it works fine. But models are trained on public data. The actually valuable stuff is increasingly private, locked behind operators and proprietary models and agents that aren't going to give it away. There are already more specialised providers than you could ever hold API keys for, and that number only goes up. At some point your agent has to go outside the walled garden and hire a stranger.

Something comes back. Was it actually the specialist model or did they run the cheapest thing available and pocket the difference? Did the model even run? You have no way to check, and neither does your agent. Multi-agent frameworks solve orchestration inside systems you control. This is the part they don't touch: verification between systems that have no reason to trust each other.

VCR is the receipt. Cryptographic proof of what ran, what went in, what came out, when, and who. Verifiable by anyone.

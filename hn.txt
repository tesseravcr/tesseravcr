  Title: Show HN: Verified Compute Receipts, a spec for agent-to-agent trust

  Text:
  A model + MCP + APIs can handle most things today. But the actually valuable
  data is increasingly scarce, already pretrained on or locked behind operators
  who aren't giving it away. When your agent needs to go beyond what it can
  access directly and transact with agents it has no relationship with, there's
  no receipt for that. No proof of what model ran, what went in, what came out,
  or whether it ran at all.

  I wrote a formal spec, built conformant implementations in Python and Rust
  (both produce identical hashes for identical inputs, that's the point), and ran
   adversarial game theory simulations against 5 attack strategies. 21 fields,
  provenance chains, royalty cascades, no token, no blockchain. MIT license.

  Demo: https://tesseravcr.github.io
  Spec and code: https://github.com/tesseravcr

  Curious whether this resonates with anyone or if I'm solving a problem that
  doesn't exist yet. Either answer is useful.
